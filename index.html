Dharamshala Trackers

Best Tracks Of Dharamshala
Triund Track

Triund is a small hill station in the Kangra district of Himachal Pradesh, India, at the base of the Dhauladhar ranges. It's 18 kilometers from Dharamshala and is known as the "crown jewel of dharamshala". Triund is a popular trekking destination, with views of the Dhauladhar ranges on one side and the Kangra Valley on the other. The trek to Triund is considered one of the best short treks in Himachal.

Thatharana

Thatharana trek is an uncharted trek but worth exploring trek in the book of the best trekking places in Himachal Pradesh. The trek location advances to the most scenic and exquisite views of the Kangra canyon a little far away from the hustle-bustle of the city where a man can silently sit in the corner and read the journal of life and recounting happenings. The trek is approximately 5 km, The trek takes 3-5 hours based on your energy, determination, and efforts.

Indrahar Pass

Indrahar Pass is a mountain pass in the Dhauladhar range of the Himalayas. It's located at an altitude of 4,342 meters (14,245 ft) above sea level, near the tourist town of Dharamshala in Himachal Pradesh. The pass forms the border between Kangra and Chamba districts




Karthani

The Karthani Trek is a moderately challenging trek located in the Dhauladhar range of Himachal Pradesh, India. It's a perfect weekend getaway for those seeking stunning Himalayan views, fresh mountain air, and a chance to experience nature's beauty The starting point for the Karthani trek is usually Mcleodganj. You can reach Mcleodganj by air, train, or bus.

kareri Lake

Kareri lake is best known for being a trekking destination in the Dhauladhars. Kareri Lake Trek is a 13 km moderate to tough trek covering 5 km forest area including mixed pine and broadleaf forests followed by grasslands interspersed with pine and deodar in the second half of the trail carved into granite rocks. There is a temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and Shakti on a hilltop overlooking the lake.

Himani Chamunda

Aadi Himani Chamunda temple is accessible via two natural valley based trek. It's around 7.7 Kilometers hilly trek from kand village which is fully loaded with numbers of dhaba and tea shops, solar lights and also with 3 water sources. From Jia Village: There is also one moderately difficult trek from Jia village.
